## Abstract

This document presents the main conceptual outline of a theoretical framework in which physical reality is described as a consistent smooth continuum.

The framework is based on the following fundamental components:

- time differential `dt`,
- Euclidean space,
- electric permittivity `Œµ(x,y,z)`,
- magnetic permeability `Œº(x,y,z)`,
- energy `E`.

In this view, photons, matter, electric charge, gravitation, and electromagnetic phenomena are explored as consequences of the interaction between energy and spatially variable electric permittivity and magnetic permeability.

## Fundamental assumption

The universe is treated as a consistent smooth continuum.

This means that physical change is continuous, finite, and bounded. There are no instantaneous physical changes, no infinite velocities, and no physically meaningful singular events occurring ‚Äúin no time‚Äù.

## Local propagation speed

The local speed of energy propagation is determined by:

`v = 1 / sqrt(Œµ Œº)`

where `Œµ` is the local electric permittivity and `Œº` is the local magnetic permeability.

In vacuum, the minimal values `Œµ0` and `Œº0` define the maximal propagation speed:

`c = 1 / sqrt(Œµ0 Œº0)`

## Photon as an energy element

A photon is modeled as a finite, smooth energy element.

It is not treated as a dimensionless point, but as a spatially finite distribution of energy moving through the continuum.

## Matter formation

Matter formation is explored as vortex formation of energy.

When high-energy photons interact in a region where energy density locally modifies `Œµ` and `Œº`, stable or semi-stable vortex structures may emerge. These structures are considered as candidates for the formation of elementary particles such as the electron and positron.

## Electric charge

Electric charge is treated as an emergent phenomenon.

In this framework, charge is not assumed as a primitive property of matter. Instead, it is explored as a consequence of the spatial distribution of energy, electric permittivity, magnetic permeability, and vortex orientation.

## Gravitation

Gravitation is explored through spatial gradients of `Œµ` and `Œº`.

A concentration of energy changes the local properties of the continuum. Since the local propagation speed depends on `Œµ` and `Œº`, photons and other energy structures follow curved paths in regions where these quantities vary spatially.
